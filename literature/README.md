# Literature Collection: Attention–Value Wedge in Digital Retail

## Literature Gap Addressed
Most prior work establishes that position, salience, and ranking affect attention-related outcomes (clicks, views). However, the literature is much less clear on whether earlier or more prominent exposure translates into economically meaningful downstream value (purchase, conditional conversion, realized demand quality). This collection addresses that gap.

## Key Gap-Relevant Findings Across Literature

1. **Position strongly affects clicks** — universal finding across all studies
2. **Position does NOT affect purchase conditional on search** — Ursu (2018), Ghose et al. (2014), the most direct evidence for the attention-value wedge
3. **Optimized rankings can reduce the wedge** — Compiani et al. (2024), Donnelly et al. (2024)
4. **Exposure→click→conversion pipeline has distinct biases at each stage** — Chen et al. (2023), Zhang et al. (2020)
5. **Attention is a scarce, allocatable resource** — Gabaix (2019), Bordalo et al. (2020), Cattaneo et al. (2023)
6. **Cross-context comparisons remain rare** — this is the core literature gap your study addresses
